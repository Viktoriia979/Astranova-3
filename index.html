Latest Space Missions:

The Ones That Have Already Took Place:

Artemis 2 (2026)

Artemis II was the first mission in over 50 years to send astronauts around the Moon and safely back to Earth. Four astronauts traveled more than one million kilometres during the mission. They tested the Orion spacecraft and its life-support systems for future Moon landings. The mission ended successfully with a splashdown in the Pacific Ocean in April 2026.

Chang'e 6 (2024)

Chang'e 6 became the first mission ever to bring back samples from the far side of the Moon. The spacecraft landed on the Moon, collected rocks and soil, and returned them to Earth. Scientists hope these samples will help explain how the Moon formed. The mission was completed successfully in June 2024.

Polaris Dawn (2024)

Polaris Dawn was a private space mission that made history with the first commercial spacewalk. The crew spent several days in orbit around Earth while testing new space technologies. They also reached one of the highest Earth orbits ever achieved by humans. The mission showed how private companies are playing a larger role in space exploration.

SLIM (2024)

SLIM, created by Japan, successfully landed on the Moon in January 2024. Its main goal was to demonstrate highly accurate lunar landing technology. Although the spacecraft landed at an unusual angle, it still completed important scientific work. The mission proved that precise Moon landings are possible.

Europa Clipper Launch (2024)

NASA launched the Europa Clipper spacecraft in October 2024 on a journey to Jupiter's moon Europa. The mission's goal is to study Europa's icy surface and underground ocean. Scientists want to learn whether the moon could support life. While the spacecraft is still travelling, its launch phase was completed successfully in 2024.

The Ones That Will Take Place In Future:

Artemis III (2027)

Artemis III aims to return astronauts to the Moon for the first time since 1972. The crew is expected to land near the Moon's south pole, where there may be ice deposits. Astronauts will conduct scientific experiments and test new technologies. The mission is an important step toward future human missions to Mars.

Dragonfly (2028)

Dragonfly is a robotic drone that will travel to Titan, Saturn's largest moon. It will fly between different locations on Titan's surface, studying its atmosphere and chemistry. Scientists hope to learn more about conditions that may resemble early Earth. The mission could provide clues about how life develops.

NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar (NISAR) (2026)

NISAR is a joint mission between NASA and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). The satellite will monitor changes in Earth's land and ice surfaces. It will help scientists study natural hazards such as earthquakes, volcanoes, and landslides. The data collected will improve our understanding of climate change.

ExoMars Rosalind Franklin Rover (2028)

The Rosalind Franklin rover will search for signs of past life on Mars. It is equipped with a drill that can collect samples from beneath the Martian surface. Scientists believe underground samples may be better protected from harsh radiation. The mission could help answer whether life ever existed on Mars.

Interstellar Probe (2030)

The Interstellar Probe is a planned mission that would travel farther than any previous spacecraft. Its goal is to study the outer edge of the Solar System and the space beyond it. Scientists hope it will provide new information about our cosmic neighborhood. If approved, it could become one of the most ambitious space missions ever attempted.
